Coffee Grinder Redesign

AME 503: Advanced Mechanical Design

A semester long project for my advanced mechanical design class that started with customer ethnography, which informed the development of customer needs and functional decomposition. After developing a few design variants, my group then developed a final product. I was responsible for initial customer ethnography, concept generation, and the CAD design of the device.

For my DFMA class, I designed a hair dryer, focusing on design for manufacturability and assembly and performed a DFMA analysis of the design. This was an individual project.

BME 405: Senior Projects - Measurement and Instrumentation

A grip strength rehabilitation device for stroke survivors that I designed and prototyped for my senior design project. I was responsible for the CAD design of the device and the manufacturing and assembly of the device.

Braille Buddy: Braille Phonics Toy

AME 504: Mechatronics Systems Design

A mechatronic system to teach Braille phonics to visually impaired children. The device utilizes solenoids to actuate Braille letters and RFID readers and tags for individual letter recognition. I was responsible for the CAD design of the device and the development of software to make the solenoids actuate to create each letter of the Braille alphabet.

Linear Motion Control System

AME 504: Mechatronics Systems Design

A closed loop control system for a lead-screw mechanism to accurately and precisely mover an indicator in 1-inch increments. I utilized Hall-effect sensors and magnets to ensure that the indictator stayed within system boundaries and to establish the system's origin (i.e., the center point of the two Hall sensors). I designed the system in Solidworks, fabricated it, and programmed the system in the Arduino IDE. The system utilized a rotary encoder and motor driver IC to control rotation of a DC motor. This was an individual project.

Keurig Product Dissection
